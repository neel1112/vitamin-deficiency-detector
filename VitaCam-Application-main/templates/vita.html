Types of vitamins

VITAMIN A

Vitamin A helps form and maintain healthy teeth, skeletal and soft tissue, mucus membranes, and skin. It is also known as retinol because it produces the pigments in the retina of the eye.

VITAMIN B

Vitamin B is a group of eight water-soluble vitamins that help the body convert food into energy. They are also known as the B-complex or B-group vitamins.

VITAMIN C

Vitamin C, also known as ascorbic acid, is a nutrient that helps the body heal, produce collagen, and absorb iron. It's also an antioxidant that helps protect cells from damage.

VITAMIN D

Without sufficient vitamin D, bones can become thin, brittle, or misshapen. Vitamin D sufficiency prevents rickets in children and osteomalacia in adults

VITAMIN E

It helps keep the immune system strong against viruses and bacteria. It helps form red blood cells and widen blood vessels to keep blood from clotting inside them. It helps the body use vitamin K. Cells also use vitamin E to interact with each other.

VITAMIN K

Vitamin K is a fat-soluble vitamin that helps your body clot blood and heal wounds. It's also important for bone health.
